Welcome to the campaign website for Jackson Meade! Thanks for stopping by, I'm glad you're here.

Results You Can Trust

If I had the honor of your vote, this would not be the first student council leadership position I have held. From early middle school through my Freshmen year, I served as President of my Student Body. I have served as the President and Vice President of many of my school's clubs, where I have learned how to work as a team member and leader.

Investment in my Community

I never thought a place like NCSSM could be possible. I've grown up in a fortunately stable environment, but I never knew the capacity for acceptance that NCSSM has shown is possible. As an elected representative, it would be my goal to continue that community of acceptance and hard work.

Dedication to my Work

From coding most of this website to building a student community on NCSSM's Campus already, my actions will be thorough and my goals will be clear.




My Platform
I have seen the kind of power that creating a group around a set of humorous ideas has within all of you. You, the student body, have created a culture rarely found anywhere else that promotes acceptance with a touch of nerdy sarcasm. I want to make sure that that kind of community is able to reach those students studying with us online and at home. I want to enable more activities like the ones I've created with my hall (a Smash Tournament and the Gregarian Federational Construct) to bring people together with more than just "speed friending."

When I came to Science and Math on Welcome Day, I almost decided not to accept. The buildings, imposing and old against the grey sky, did not show the kind of warmth our school has to offer. It occurred to me that giving Beall and Reynolds a good power wash might help with that, and with your support, I can create programs that would cover something so superficial yet extremely important. I want to create more beautification committees in student government to keep Watts Hospital looking as new as its rising little sibling, the Morganton Campus.

A Little About Me

Hi. My name is Jackson Meade, and I attend the North Carolina School of Science and Mathematics, and I'm running for Junior Senator.

But who am I beneath that line?

I'm from Charlotte, North Carolina. Like the Triangle, it's a big place, and I hadn't met anyone in North Carolina outside of Charlotte (excluding family) because of it. In my first year of High School, I joined a chapter of the Technology Student Association. It allowed me to meet people from all over the state, and it was great to find so many like-minded people from places I hadn't ever heard of. That's mostly what drew me to Science and Math: You.

I have a brother, Cavan (pictured behind), who is two years younger than I am. I've always had to be a leader, though I haven't always lived up to it. But nobody's perfect, and being an older brother has taught me many invaluable lessons about resolving conflicts and modeling the way forward.

I'm most uneasy when I'm idle. You'll usually find me doing something, from drawing (though I can't boast wonderful technique) to coding. I hope I can apply that restlessness to some change at Science and Math with your support.

Leadership Experience

As soon as I started High School in 2016, I joined the Student Council. I served as Freshmen Body President for South Mecklenburg High School, where I learned how to lead a school while holding a lower-class position. As a Junior, I want to make just as much of an impact as the senior senators, and I have the experience to make that happen.

While at South Meck, I served as President of my Technology Student Association Chapter, and led our team to a first-place win at the State Competition and a fourth-place win at the National Competition. TSA Stresses leadership, and I attended leadership seminars that drilled Robert's Rules of Order into me. I can hold a parliamentary session, as we do here at Science and Math.

I served on a diversity board at my home high school, where I learned the importance of creating a diverse leadership board. Make sure to consider how you can elect a diverse senate, as I have found that a diverse board is often extremely effective in the most stressful areas.

STRATEGIC PLAN

This timeline outlines the first few actions I intend to take if elected to the post of Junior Senator.

September 19th-21st, 2018

With your support, I hope to be elected to the position of Junior Senator to begin my work on the community. Regardless of whether I am elected, I will strive to make NCSSM a better place for its students.

September 24th-27th, 2018

With the help of administration, I plan to hold a session with NCSSM online and Summer Ventures Juniors to bolster our outreach committees and maintain our commitment to reaching as many NC Students as possible.

October 3rd-17th

Organize an External Campus Improvement Committee and immediately work on reviving the campus gardens and cleaning the buildings' exteriors. Hold a Mental Health Seminar centered around the impact of a living environment on its students.

October 15th-24th

Discuss Halloween activities that could be used to raise money for Durham Community Outreach

October 24th-29th

Create designated environments similar to the mindfulness area of Royall to encourage deep studying and relaxation immediately prior to exams.
